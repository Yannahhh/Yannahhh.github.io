--- layout: publication authors: - Lingdan Shao - Zhe Chu - Xi Chen - Yanna Lin - Wei Zeng awards: - Best Paper Honorable Mention Award highlight: true link: https://lingdan33.github.io/bayesmvlayout/ pdf: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12650-021-00781-z image: ../assets/img/publication/shao2021MVBayesian.png tags: - Visualization - Multiple Views title: "Modeling layout design for multiple-view visualization via Bayesian inference" type: - Conference - Journal venue: ChinaVis venue_tags: - Visualization venue_url: http://www.chinavis.org/2022/ year: 2021 --- General visualization tools typically require manual specification of views: analysts must select data variables and then choose which transformations and visual encodings to apply. These decisions often involve both domain and visualization design expertise, and may impose a tedious specification process that impedes exploration. In this paper, we seek to complement manual chart construction with interactive navigation of a gallery of automatically-generated visualizations. We contribute Voyager, a mixed-initiative system that supports faceted browsing of recommended charts chosen according to statistical and perceptual measures. We describe Voyager's architecture, motivating design principles, and methods for generating and interacting with visualization recommendations. In a study comparing Voyager to a manual visualization specification tool, we find that Voyager facilitates exploration of previously unseen data and leads to increased data variable coverage. We then distill design implications for visualization tools, in particular the need to balance rapid exploration and targeted question-answering.
